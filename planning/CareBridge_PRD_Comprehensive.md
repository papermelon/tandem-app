# Product Requirements Document (PRD): CareBridge

**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Final for AI-Assisted Development  
**Target Event:** The Good Hack 2026 (Quality of Life Track)

---

## 1. Executive Summary
**CareBridge** is a mobile-first web application designed to solve the "family coordination" gap in the ASEAN caregiver landscape. While current apps focus on professional service booking, CareBridge targets the **unpaid informal caregiver circle** (siblings, family, and Migrant Domestic Workers). It centralizes fragmented medical data into a single, AI-verified **"Ground Truth"** and uses a physical-presence **QR-code protocol** to ensure seamless knowledge transfer during handovers, reducing primary caregiver burnout.

## 2. Problem Statement & Market Gaps
Based on competitive analysis of the ASEAN landscape:
* **Coordination Gap:** There is no integrated tool in ASEAN that allows family members and MDWs to share a single live care record (meds, vitals, doctor's instructions) [cite: 171, 174].
* **The Handover Deficit:** Most knowledge transfer is paper-based or verbal, leading to "discharge confusion" and missed follow-ups [cite: 219, 226].
* **Language Barriers:** Existing apps are often English-only, making them inaccessible to the FDWs who do the majority of the day-to-day work [cite: 175, 319].
* **Inclusivity Issues:** Government tools like HealthHub often exclude FIN holders (foreigners/FDWs), creating a digital silo [cite: 237].

## 3. Core Product Features (The "Two Branches")

### 3.1 Branch 1: The "Trust Bridge" Handover (Onboarding)
* **Physical Presence Verification:** A session-specific QR code generated by the departing caregiver. The incoming caregiver must scan it to initiate the handover, ensuring both are physically present.
* **Guided Training Checklist:** A mandatory UI walkthrough where the new caregiver reviews the current "Ground Truth" instructions (Meds, Mobility, Mood).
* **Reverse-Verification Translation:** Uses the **SEA-LION model** to translate English instructions into Tagalog, Bahasa, or Burmese. The original English is always displayed alongside for verification.
* **The "Care Wrapped" Receipt:** A summary generated for the departing caregiver confirming the successful transfer, supporting the "Respite Metric."

### 3.2 Branch 2: The "Ground Truth" Instruction Engine
* **Low-Effort Ingestion:**
    * **Photo-to-Field:** Support for uploading photos of medication Bill of Materials (BOM) or discharge summaries, parsed by AI into structured fields for verification.
    * **Voice Memo Capture:** Recording features for doctor consultations, summarized into the care history.
* **Conflict Resolution Logic:** The system explicitly flags discrepancies between official medical logs and family narrative notes (e.g., preference for medicine time), requiring the primary caregiver to set the "Ground Truth."
* **Data Lifecycle:** A permanent, versioned archive of all care history. During Q&A sessions, the AI highlights changes (e.g., "Dosage increased from 5mg to 10mg").

### 3.3 Active Care Coordination
* **Shared Task Calendar:** A collaborative workspace similar to Slack or Splitwise for assigning and claiming daily tasks (Vitals, Meds, Hygiene).
* **Narrative Care Diary:** A family-editable narrative feed for tracking non-clinical observations (mood, appetite, quirks).

## 4. Technical Specifications for AI Coding (Claude Code/Cursor)

### 4.1 Architecture & Privacy
* **Platform:** Mobile-first PWA (Progressive Web App).
* **Persistence:** Use **IndexedDB** for client-side storage to address privacy concerns and ensure offline access in hospitals/older HDBs.
* **Access Control:** "Circle of Care" model. No public sign-ups; access is granted via the Primary Caregiver’s QR scan.

### 4.2 AI Integration
* **Model:** SEA-LION LLM for localized translation; Vision-LLM for OCR/Parsing of BOM photos.
* **Safety Guardrails:** Manual "Verification Checkbox" for all AI-parsed fields before they become "Ground Truth."

## 5. Success Metrics (Hackathon Rubric Alignment)
* **Social Impact:** Increase in the **"Respite Confidence Score"** (1-5) reported by primary caregivers after a handover.
* **Quality of Life:** Reduction in "medication errors" and "communication friction" within the household dyad [cite: 262].
* **Technical Innovation:** Use of localized LLMs (SEA-LION) and physical-verification onboarding (QR).
