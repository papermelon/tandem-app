Tandem Hackathon Demo Prep

Judge Background and Pitch Considerations

A practical briefing for tailoring Tandem's demo to three judges: patient advocacy and behavioural health, ageing and mental health social impact, and public-good healthtech engineering.

Prepared May 10, 2026. Based on public research and Tandem repo context.

Recommended positioning: Tandem is care coordination infrastructure for family caregivers. It turns scattered notes, appointments, documents, and family messages into shared context, reviewed tasks, handovers, and accountability. It is not a diagnostic tool and does not replace clinicians or caregivers.

What To Lead With

Core story

After a clinic visit, instructions sit across WhatsApp, PDFs, voice notes, and memory. One sibling assumes another handled transport, medication reminders are unclear, and the primary caregiver silently becomes the project manager.

Demo proof

Show Tandem capturing a care item, extracting the important actions, assigning work across the care circle, showing workload imbalance, and generating a handover for a family member stepping in.

Demo Spine

Step What to show Why it lands
1. Fragmented care Doctor memo, family note, or Telegram capture Names the real coordination failure before introducing technology.
2. AI capture Plain-English summary plus extracted tasks Shows immediate reduction in admin burden, with human review.
3. Shared ownership Assign or claim transport, admin, medication reminder, check-in Moves from "Rachel remembers everything" to distributed care.
4. Load visibility Care-load view showing imbalance Makes invisible caregiver burden visible and actionable.
5. Handover 7-day brief for a sibling returning after being away Shows continuity of care without making the lead caregiver repeat herself.
6. Safety close No diagnosis, source-linked summaries, review-before-save Pre-empts clinical safety, privacy, and AI-overreach concerns.

Judge-Specific Guidance

Soh Lai Yee
Patient advocacy, behavioural health, person- and family-centred care
Care partnership

Relevant background

  • President of the Society of Behavioural Health Singapore.
  • Group Chief Patient Officer at NUHS, leading patient advocacy and person- and family-centred care work.
  • Associated with initiatives involving patient, caregiver, and community participation in care design, including the Patient and Family Advocacy Council for Tengah General and Community Hospital.

How to pitch to her

  • Say "caregivers as partners in care," not "AI manages the family."
  • Highlight human review, plain-English summaries, and family consent.
  • Frame Tandem as a behavioural nudge system that helps the whole care circle act earlier.
Reference NUHS Caregivers' Day and TGCH Patient and Family Advocacy Council: patients, caregivers, advocates, and residents helping shape care practices, design, feedback, health education, and family support.
Features to highlight AI-assisted capture, handover briefs, meeting summaries, workload visibility, review-before-save, medication reminders as notes only.
Refinements Add "patient/caregiver reviewed" status, clearer consent language, source-linked summaries, and a "what changed since last visit" view.
Jerome Yuen
Ageing communities, loneliness reduction, mental health access, nonprofit growth
Dignity and connection

Relevant background

  • Chief Growth Partner at The Therapy Platform, which presents itself around accessible and secure counselling.
  • Former CEO of Cycling Without Age Singapore, including work on senior social connection and VR trishaw experiences.
  • Has nonprofit and fundraising experience, including Club Rainbow / Ride for Rainbows and World Vision Singapore.

How to pitch to him

  • Show that Tandem helps families show up, not just close tasks.
  • Include one emotional support task: call after rehab, visit, or check in after a difficult appointment.
  • Talk about dignity, reassurance, and reducing loneliness without making the older adult passive.
Reference Cycling Without Age's senior engagement work and Virtual Generations VR rides were designed around social isolation, mobility limits, reminiscence, and emotional wellbeing.
Features to highlight Check-in tasks, family engagement loop, voice capture, warm timeline, handovers that help distant family members re-enter the care circle.
Refinements Separate clinical/admin tasks from social connection tasks, add compassionate notification copy, and show caregiver appreciation or contribution visibility.
Nicolas Spano
Public-good engineering, healthtech delivery, practical user-centred systems
Feasibility and impact

Relevant background

  • Listed by OGP as Head of HealthTech.
  • OGP HealthTech work includes Bright, Care360, Health Appointment System, Referral Exchange, Rooster, and Social360.
  • OGP emphasizes rapid prototyping, real users, public-good delivery, security, and measurable outcomes.

How to pitch to him

  • Be precise about what is already built versus v2 roadmap.
  • Show workflow reliability: deterministic demo mode, review queues, task state, and auditability.
  • Name metrics: handover time, extraction review rate, missed-task reduction, load imbalance reduction.
Reference OGP HealthTech products solve coordination problems across appointments, referrals, social work, and clinical messaging. Tandem is analogous at the family-care layer.
Features to highlight Next.js/Supabase/OpenAI stack, Telegram ingestion, mock fallback, structured task/timeline data model, handover generation, safety boundaries.
Refinements Add source citations, a visible privacy model, simple audit trail, and one metrics screen or slide showing expected impact.

Feature Mapping

Tandem feature Why it matters Best judge resonance
Telegram, voice, document, and web capture Meets families where information already lives instead of forcing a new workflow. Soh: access and family participation. Nicolas: practical adoption.
AI extraction with review-before-save Reduces admin work while keeping humans in control. Soh: patient safety and agency. Nicolas: AI guardrails.
Task board and ownership Turns vague "someone should do this" into visible responsibility. All three.
Care-load visibility Surfaces the invisible burden carried by the primary caregiver. Soh: caregiver advocacy. Jerome: dignity and wellbeing.
Handover brief Lets backup caregivers step in without interrogating the primary caregiver. Soh: continuity of care. Nicolas: clear workflow value.
Meeting summaries Captures decisions, open questions, and follow-ups from doctor or family meetings. Soh: person-centred decisions. Nicolas: structured records.
Care signals and check-ins Moves the product from record keeping toward timely family action. Jerome: connection and loneliness. Nicolas: signal quality questions.
